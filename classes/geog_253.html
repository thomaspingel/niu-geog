GEOG 253: Environment and Society

Keywords

General Education (Nature and Technology), Natural Environment Systems

Prerequisites

None

Course Description

Introduction to the study of human-environment interactions from a geographic perspective, with emphasis on the role of humans in changing the face of the earth. Fundamentals of environmental science as well as global and local issues related to human population growth, agriculture, water resources, biodiversity, forest resources, energy use, climate change, and environmental health.

Course Objectives

  • Describe the impact and issues of human population growth and economic activity on natural resources and environmental systems.
  • Explain how political and economic decisions can affect environmental systems (political ecology).
  • Describe important natural resources (and extractive technologies) of the past present and potentially, the future.
  • Describe both potential and working solutions to environmental problems.
  • Explain basic geographic themes and concepts related to environmental issues.
  • Describe the impact of economic activity and resource exploitation on human health.

Course Format

One midterm and final exam, project with annotated bibliography, various assignments, and term paper.
