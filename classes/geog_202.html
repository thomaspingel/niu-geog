GEOG 202: World Regional Geography

Keywords

General Education (Society and Culture)

Prerequisites

None

Course Description

Geography 202 is an introduction to the geographical and historical processes that have shaped different world regions. Its focus is on the physical landscapes, cultural attributes and economic/political forces distinctive to each region, as well as the interconnectivity of these factors within the world system. The course moves through an examination of each world region (Europe, Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, etc.) discussing the particular characteristics of each, while connecting how each relates to the others, and the whole.

Course Objectives

  • Learn the locations of all of the countries
  • Develop an in-depth understanding of the G20 Major Economies
  • Learn how history, economics, politics, and culture shape countries and regions
  • Understand the interrelationships and interdependence between counties and regions
  • Learn to read and produce maps and graphics related to World Regional Geography
  • Improve graphical literacy and quantitative reasoning

Course Format

Lecture format with course support in BlackBoard. Evaluation methods include in-class exams, in-class and online quizzes, and writing assignments using geographic data and maps.
